Academic Experience

Explore my journey through various academic roles and research endeavors.

Current Position

Since October 2023, I have been serving as a researcher at the Royal Military Academy in Brussels, Belgium, focusing on underwater acoustics and sonar signal processing.

Previous Academic Roles

Prior to my current position, I held several academic roles in Colombia, including:

  • Assistant Professor at Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, Universidad Militar Nueva Granada, and Universidad Sergio Arboleda, teaching subjects related to engineering, data analysis, robotics, and artificial intelligence.
  • Research Assistant in the Davinci research group at Universidad Militar Nueva Granada, working on modular robotics and bioinformatics projects.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on fostering an integrative approach that combines theoretical knowledge with practical application. I aim to inspire critical thinking and problem-solving skills in my students, encouraging them to explore interdisciplinary connections and stay abreast of emerging technologies.

Research Projects

Throughout my academic career, I have engaged in various research projects, including:

  • Modular Robotics: Designed and simulated reconfigurable modular robots, contributing to publications in international conferences.
  • Bioinformatics: Developed methods for generating pseudoknotted RNA structures using combinatorial approaches during my master's studies.
  • Underwater Acoustics: Currently working on the detection and classification of buried man-made objects in underwater environments using low-frequency sonar data and physics-informed machine learning.

Awards and Recognitions

In recognition of my academic excellence, I was awarded the "Mario Galán Gómez" scholarship from Ecopetrol in 2008. Additionally, I received several tuition-free semesters between 2008 and 2012. I have also been invited to present my research at various international conferences and symposiums.
