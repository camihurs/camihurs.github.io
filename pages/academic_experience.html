Current Position

Since October 2023, I have been serving as a researcher at the Royal Military Academy in Brussels, Belgium. My work focuses on Sub-Bottom Scanning Sonar (SBSS) for underwater buried target detection, utilizing low-frequency SONAR data and Physics-Informed Machine Learning techniques. This role allows me to integrate my expertise in mechatronics, telecommunications, and applied mathematics to address complex challenges in underwater acoustics.

Previous Academic Roles

Prior to my current position, I held several academic roles in Colombia:

  • Universidad Militar Nueva Granada (UMNG) – Undergraduate Professor (January 2023 - September 2023): I taught Digital Circuits, focusing on the principles and applications of digital electronics.
  • Pontificia Universidad Javeriana – Undergraduate and Master's Professor (June 2021 - June 2022): I instructed courses such as Computing for Artificial Intelligence (Python), Computational Mathematics (Python), and Introduction to Programming (C++), emphasizing the development of computational thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • Universidad Sergio Arboleda – Undergraduate Professor (2019 - 2022): I taught courses including Big Data Marketing (R), Data Analysis in Social Networks (Python), Quantitative Methods, and Applied Statistics, integrating data analysis techniques with marketing strategies and social network analysis.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on fostering an integrative approach that bridges theoretical concepts with practical applications. I aim to inspire students to develop critical thinking skills and approach problems with creativity and analytical rigor. By creating an engaging learning environment, I encourage students to connect academic knowledge with real-world scenarios, preparing them for professional challenges.

Research Projects

Throughout my academic career, I have engaged in various research projects that reflect my interdisciplinary interests:

  • Modular Robotics and 3D Printing: During my bachelor's degree and entrepreneurial endeavors, I explored the design and development of modular robotic systems and advanced 3D printing techniques.
  • Bioinformatics: In my master's program, I focused on generating pseudoknotted RNA skeletons using combinatorial methods involving Catalan objects and irreducible chord diagrams.
  • Underwater Acoustics: Currently, my PhD research involves detecting and classifying buried man-made objects in underwater environments using low-frequency SONAR data and Physics-Informed Machine Learning.
